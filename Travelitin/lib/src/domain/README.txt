The Domain layer encapsulates the core business logic and entities of the application. It is independent of any external frameworks or technologies, focusing solely on the application's business rules.

Key Components:
- Entities: Classes that represent the core data models and business objects within the application.
- Use Cases: Classes that define specific business operations or actions that the application can perform.
- Repositories: Abstract classes that define the contract for data retrieval and storage operations.

Responsibilities:
- Define the core business entities and their relationships.
- Implement the application's business rules and operations.
- Provide interfaces for data access through repositories.
- Ensure that the business logic is independent of external frameworks.
